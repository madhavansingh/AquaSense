# AquaGuard Knowledge Base

This document provides simple, farmer-friendly explanations for common fish diseases detected by AquaGuard. 

## 1. Aeromoniasis
*   **Short Description:** A common bacterial infection that attacks the skin and internal organs.
*   **Visible Symptoms:** Open sores, deep ulcers, red spots on the body, rotting fins, or a swollen, bloated belly.
*   **Causes:** Stress caused by poor water quality, overcrowding, or sudden temperature drops.
*   **Early Warning Signs:** Fish swimming sluggishly near the surface, loss of appetite, or minor reddish tints appearing on the fins.
*   **Impact:** **High.** It spreads very quickly and can kill many fish in a short time if ignored.

## 2. Gill Disease
*   **Short Description:** An infection or irritation that damages the gills, making it hard for fish to breathe.
*   **Visible Symptoms:** Gills look swollen, pale, or covered in extra mucus. Fish gasping at the water surface.
*   **Causes:** High ammonia/nitrite levels in the water, bacterial infections, or heavy mud/silt in the pond.
*   **Early Warning Signs:** Fish gathering around water inlets or aerators, breathing heavily.
*   **Impact:** **High.** Fish can suffocate rapidly if water quality isn't fixed immediately.

## 3. Red Disease (Epizootic Ulcerative Syndrome)
*   **Short Description:** A severe fungal and bacterial disease causing deep, spreading wounds.
*   **Visible Symptoms:** Large, reddish, painful-looking ulcers that eat away the skin and muscle.
*   **Causes:** Fungal spores in the water, usually triggered after heavy rains or when water temperature drops.
*   **Early Warning Signs:** Small red spots or dark, raised bumps on the skin before they break open into sores.
*   **Impact:** **Extreme.** Very contagious and deadly. Can wipe out entire ponds.

## 4. Fungal (Saprolegniasis)
*   **Short Description:** A fungal infection that looks like cotton growing on the fish.
*   **Visible Symptoms:** Fluffy, white or gray cotton-like tufts on the skin, fins, or gills.
*   **Causes:** Fungi attack fish that are already weakened by rough handling, cold water, or other wounds.
*   **Early Warning Signs:** Small white patches appearing on areas where scales are missing or fins are torn.
*   **Impact:** **Moderate.** Usually only affects already weakened fish, but can be fatal if the fungus covers the gills.

## 5. Parasitic Infection
*   **Short Description:** Tiny bugs (like lice, anchor worms, or ich) attaching to the fish and feeding on them.
*   **Visible Symptoms:** Fish scratching against rocks or the pond bottom. Visible worms attached to the skin, or tiny white dots (like salt grains) all over the body.
*   **Causes:** Introducing infected new fish into the pond without quarantine, or poor water flow.
*   **Early Warning Signs:** Restless swimming, flashing (rubbing sides on the bottom), and clamped fins.
*   **Impact:** **Moderate to High.** Parasites weaken fish and open wounds for bacteria to enter.

## 6. Viral Infection
*   **Short Description:** Internal viruses that affect the organs. Hardest to treat directly.
*   **Visible Symptoms:** Bulging eyes, strange swimming patterns (spinning), dark body color, or bleeding under the skin.
*   **Causes:** Spreads from infected fish, birds, or contaminated equipment.
*   **Early Warning Signs:** Sudden, unexplained deaths of a few fish without obvious outward wounds.
*   **Impact:** **Extreme.** There are no medicines for viruses. Prevention and isolation are the only ways to stop it.

## 7. Healthy
*   **Short Description:** Fish show no signs of external disease.
*   **Visible Symptoms:** Bright, clear eyes. Intact fins. Smooth, shiny scales. Normal, active swimming and strong feeding behavior.
*   **Causes:** Good water management, proper feeding, and low stress.
*   **Early Warning Signs:** None. Keep doing what you are doing!
*   **Impact:** **Positive.** Optimal growth and yield.
